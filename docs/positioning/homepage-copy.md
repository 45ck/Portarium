## Problem section

### Heading

AI can do a lot. The question is: should it?

### Body

AI agents can plan, draft, and call tools. But in production, the real questions are:

- Who allowed this action?
- What rules were applied?
- What evidence existed at the time?
- What can be replayed, audited, or rolled back?

Most agent tools skip these questions. Approvals get bolted on after the fact. Evidence is scattered across logs. Nobody can explain who approved what or why.

Portarium exists to close that gap.

---

## How it works (3-step)

### Step 1: AI proposes

The AI agent drafts an action — send this email, update this record, deploy this change.

### Step 2: Portarium checks

Is this allowed? Is it risky? Does it need a human? Portarium evaluates the rules and decides: auto-approve, queue for human review, or block.

### Step 3: Evidence is kept

Every decision, every action, every outcome is recorded and linked together. Full paper trail. Always reviewable.

## Feature cards

### 1. Rules before actions

AI is checked against your rules before it is allowed to do anything. No more hoping the prompt was good enough.

### 2. Human approval built in

Risky actions pause and wait for a human decision. Approval is part of the process, not an afterthought.

### 3. Full paper trail

Every action, decision, and outcome is recorded. You can always see what AI tried to do, what was approved, and what changed.

### 4. Works with your existing tools

Portarium connects to business systems you already use. Your software stays the system of record.

### 5. Gradual trust

Start with full human oversight. Widen autonomy as confidence grows. Four trust levels from fully manual to fully automatic.

### 6. Sits above any agent

Not a replacement for your AI. The safety layer on top of it. Works with any agent framework.
